Design principles

Four tenets that guide every visual and interaction decision in DWDS. Used to settle trade-offs and keep the brand consistent across surfaces.

Human-centric

People-first, empathetic design.

Decisions begin with the person on the other side of the screen — what they're trying to do, what they're worried about, what would make them feel seen. Empathy outranks elegance.

Clear

Confident, simple, and intuitive communication.

Plain words, generous whitespace, and a single point of focus per screen. We earn the user's attention by saying less and saying it precisely.

Balanced

Harmony between function and emotion.

Function comes first, but it is never alone. Type, color, and motion are tuned so the system feels considered, not clinical — useful, but with warmth.

Delightful

Warm, sophisticated visuals that inspire joy.

A small accent of lime, a hand-drawn icon, a sentence with rhythm. The brand earns trust through competence and rewards attention with quiet moments of pleasure.
